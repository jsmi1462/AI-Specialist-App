The Gray Area

Three scenarios. No obvious answers. About 10 minutes.

We all know that AI is a constant presence in schools.

Maybe you use it regularly, maybe you don't. Most of your teachers probably use it too. The question isn't "AI: yes or no?" — that isn't the debate anymore.

The more interesting question is: what are you actually trying to get out of school? And how does the easy route intersect with that?

This isn't a quiz with right answers baked in. It's a few scenarios — things that could actually happen to you this year — where you make choices and see where they lead. There isn't any preaching. No pop-up that says "WRONG! Academic integrity means..." These situations are genuinely complicated, and we think you're smart enough to figure out where your own lines are.

The thing no one says out loud: AI can't make the hard part of school easier. The hard part of school was never the writing — it was figuring out what you actually think. The essay was just the proof of work.

AI can absolutely help you learn, organize, and edit. It can also let you skip the uncomfortable stretch of not-knowing-yet. That stretch is where most of the actual learning happens.

Want to talk through any of this with a teacher, counselor, or the Student Council? That's the point.
